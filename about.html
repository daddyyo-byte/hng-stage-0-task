About Me

My journey, reflections, and aspirations

Bio

I'm Dami Bakare, a passionate software developer dedicated to creating meaningful and accessible web experiences. My journey into tech began with a curiosity about how things work on the internet, and it has evolved into a commitment to building solutions that make a difference.

I believe in the power of clean code, thoughtful design, and continuous learning. Every project is an opportunity to grow, and every challenge is a chance to innovate. Beyond coding, I'm deeply interested in the human side of technology—how we can use our skills to create inclusive digital spaces that serve everyone.

Goals in This Program

Participating in this program represents a significant step in my development journey. Here are my key goals:

  • Master Modern Web Development

    I want to deepen my understanding of frontend and backend technologies, learning not just how to write code, but how to write scalable, maintainable, and performant applications.

  • Build Real-World Projects

    Theory is important, but practical experience is invaluable. I aim to create projects that solve real problems and can be showcased in my portfolio to demonstrate my skills to potential employers or clients.

  • Collaborate and Network

    I'm excited to connect with fellow developers, learn from their experiences, and contribute to a supportive community. Collaboration often leads to the best learning outcomes.

  • Enhance Problem-Solving Skills

    Every bug, every challenge is an opportunity to improve my analytical thinking and approach to problem-solving. I want to become more confident in tackling complex technical issues.

Areas of Low Confidence

Growth requires honesty about where we need improvement. Here are areas where I'm still building confidence:

  • Backend Architecture

    While I'm comfortable with frontend development, designing scalable backend systems with proper database optimization and API architecture is still a learning area for me.

  • Testing and Test-Driven Development

    I understand the importance of testing, but writing comprehensive unit, integration, and end-to-end tests is something I need more practice with. TDD is a mindset I'm working to fully adopt.

  • Performance Optimization

    While I can build functional applications, optimizing them for speed and efficiency—especially at scale—is an area I want to master better.

Extra Thoughts

Technology is more than just code—it's about people. As I progress in my career, I'm increasingly aware of the responsibility we have as developers to create accessible, inclusive, and ethical technology.

I'm passionate about web accessibility because I believe the internet should be usable by everyone, regardless of their abilities. Every project I work on, I ask myself: "Who might I be excluding with this design or implementation?" This mindset has made me a better developer and a more empathetic person.

I'm also excited about the intersection of technology and education. I believe in paying it forward—sharing knowledge, mentoring others, and contributing to open source. The developer community has given me so much, and I want to give back.

Finally, I'm learning to balance ambition with patience. Growth isn't always linear, and that's okay. Some days I feel like I can conquer any coding challenge; other days, basic syntax feels overwhelming. Both experiences are part of the journey, and I'm learning to embrace them all.
